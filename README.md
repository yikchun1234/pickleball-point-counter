🎾 Pickleball Point Counter

A minimalist, Muji-inspired Pickleball Point Counter built with React & Tailwind. Tracks Standard, 14-Point, and Custom rally scoring. Features a visual rally timeline, voice score announcer, local match history, and supports 10 languages. PWA-ready and runs entirely as a single HTML file. Perfect for tracking matches on the go!

✨ Features

Zen, Muji-Inspired UI: Clean, distraction-free interface with smooth animations and interactive dot-matrix backgrounds. Seamlessly adapts to your device's Light & Dark modes.

Multiple Rule Sets: - Standard Scoring: 0-0 (Singles) or 0-0-2 (Doubles) rules, play to 11, win by 2.

14-Point Rule: Rally scoring to 15, with an optional "Must Serve to Win" sudden-death toggle at 14-14.

Custom Rules: Fully adjust the 'Play To' score, 'Win By' margin, and toggle 'Serve to Score' on or off.

Voice Announcer & Haptics: Built-in Text-to-Speech acts as your digital referee, announcing the score, second servers, and side-outs out loud. Subtle haptic vibrations provide tactile feedback during intense matches.

Visual Rally Timeline: A beautiful visual heatmap is generated for every match to track exactly how the game unfolded, point by point.

Match History & Stats Dashboard: Automatically saves completed matches to your browser's local storage. View your overall win rate, average match duration, and review past game timelines.

Smart QoL Features: Select your starting server, get prompted to switch sides at half-points, resume accidentally closed games, or record team retreats.

Multi-Language Support: Fully translated into 10 languages (English, 简体中文, 繁體中文, Bahasa Melayu, Español, Français, 日本語, 한국어, Deutsch, Português).

PWA Ready: Install it directly to your iOS or Android home screen to use it offline like a native mobile app.

Zero-Build Setup: The entire application runs natively in a single index.html file using CDNs. No Node.js, Webpack, or Vite required!

🚀 How to Use (For Players)

Playing a Match

Open the app and tap the screen to start.

Select your match format (Singles or Doubles) and your rule set (Standard, 14-Point Rule, or Custom).

(Optional) Customize the match title, enter team names, and choose who serves first.

During the game, simply tap the Team 1 or Team 2 buttons to award a point or side-out based on the active rules.

Made a mistake? Hit the Undo button in the top right to reverse the last action.

Viewing History & Stats

On the main menu, click the History (clock) or Stats (chart) icons in the top right corner.

The History drawer reveals all your past matches, final scores, and the complete point-by-point visual timeline for each game.

You can also easily share your match results to social media or messaging apps directly from the History tab.

Installing on your Phone

Android / Chrome: Scroll to the bottom of the main menu and click "Install as a native app".

iOS / Safari: Tap the "Share" icon at the bottom of your Safari screen and select "Add to Home Screen".
